# F-001.5 ‚ÄĒ Role-Based Access Gating ESO Trust Levels

> Product Feature Specification. Part of EPIC-001 (Enterprise Workspace). References engineering assets; does not modify them. No code, React, or API authoring.

---

## Problem Statement

Autonomy in HP Enterprise Brain is governed by a trust ladder (`observe | suggest | approve | autonomous`, ESO Block 5). If trust levels are not bound to roles, any person could operate an ESO at a higher autonomy than authorized ‚ÄĒ a safety and compliance failure. Access control must gate the *effective autonomy* an executor may exercise.

## Business Goal

Bind ESO trust levels (`executorPolicy.trustLevels`, Block 5) to roles so that a person's role determines the maximum autonomy they (or their delegated executors) may exercise. This is the policy foundation for human-in-the-loop governance (EPIC-007) and safe execution (EPIC-008).

## User Personas

- **Tenant Administrator** ‚ÄĒ configures role ‚Üí trust ceilings.
- **Org Lead / Manager** ‚ÄĒ tailors trust ceilings for team roles.
- **Decision Makers / Executors** ‚ÄĒ operate within their authorized ceiling.

## Business Flow

1. Administrator maps each `Role` to a trust ceiling per executor/ESO context (aligned to Block 5 `trustLevels`).
2. When a person (via role) is selected to run or decide on an ESO, the system computes their **effective autonomy** = min(role trust ceiling, ESO `trustLevels` ceiling).
3. Actions above the effective ceiling require human decision (EPIC-007); actions at `autonomous` may self-execute (EPIC-008).
4. All trust computations and decisions are tenant-scoped and append-only (EPIC-007 ledger).

## Inputs

- Role ‚Üí trust ceiling mappings (per executor class: human | agent | software | hybrid).
- ESO `executorPolicy.trustLevels` (Block 5) ‚ÄĒ the contract ceiling.
- Person's assigned roles (F-001.3).

## Outputs

- Effective autonomy bound per person/role/ESO.
- Gating decision: allow / require-human / deny.
- Audit record of trust computation.

## Intelligence Logic

- Effective autonomy ‚Č§ ceiling, where ceiling = min(role mapping, ESO `trustLevels`). This implements the recommended model in `contracts/eso/eso.schema.yaml`: `trustLevel` IS the ceiling; runtime computes effective autonomy ‚Č§ ceiling using confidence + capability maturity.
- Per-step executor class (Block 4) is evaluated against the per-step allowed classes (Block 5 `allowedExecutorClasses`) before trust is applied.
- Gate conditions may tighten the ceiling at runtime (e.g., low case confidence ‚Üí drop to `suggest`).

- `contracts/eso/eso.schema.yaml` **Block 4 `procedure.steps[]`** ‚ÄĒ per-step `executorClass` resolved before trust gating.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Each role can be mapped to a trust ceiling per executor class, tenant-scoped.
2. Effective autonomy is computed as ‚Č§ min(role ceiling, ESO `trustLevels` ceiling) ‚ÄĒ never exceeds either.
3. Actions above the effective ceiling are blocked from autonomous execution and routed to human decision (EPIC-007).
4. Trust computations are recorded and auditable; no rewrite of the ESO `trustLevels` is permitted (¬ß5.5 envelope).
