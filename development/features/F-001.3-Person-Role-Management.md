# F-001.3 — Person & Role Management

> Product Feature Specification. Part of EPIC-001 (Enterprise Workspace). References engineering assets; does not modify them. No code, React, or API authoring.

---

## Problem Statement

The system needs to know *who* its users are and *what* they are authorized to do. Without managed `Person` and `Role` entities, there is no identity to attribute evidence to, no subject for personalization (EPIC-009), and no basis for role-based access that gates ESO autonomy (EPIC-007/008).

## Business Goal

Provide governed management of people and roles within a tenant, including assignment of roles to persons, so that identity, attribution, and authorization are established once and reused by every downstream Epic.

## User Personas

- **Tenant Administrator** — creates persons, defines roles, assigns roles.
- **Org Lead / Manager** — assigns roles within their unit.
- **Individual Contributor** — the person record; subject of personalization.

## Business Flow

1. Administrator creates a `Person` (identity) within the tenant.
2. Administrator defines `Role`(s) (e.g., Investigator, Executor, Reviewer, Admin).
3. Roles are assigned to persons, optionally scoped to an `OrgUnit` (F-001.2).
4. Each `Person`/`Role` carries `tenantId` (mandatory).
5. Role assignments resolve to permissions and a trust ceiling consumed by EPIC-007/008.

## Inputs

- Person identity (name, external IdP reference).
- Role definitions (name, permission set, default trust ceiling).
- Person ↔ Role assignments (with optional unit scope).

## Outputs

- `Person` nodes (id, tenantId) per `graph/migrations/001_constraints.cypher`.
- `Role` nodes (id, tenantId).
- Assignment edges (Person → Role, optionally Role → OrgUnit).

## Intelligence Logic

- A person's effective permissions = union of assigned roles.
- A person's trust ceiling for a given executor/ESO is derived from their roles' trust levels (F-001.5), bounding autonomy in EPIC-008.
- Identity attributes feed per-person `memory.scope` personalization in EPIC-009.

## Related Contracts

- `graph/migrations/001_constraints.cypher` — `Person` (`person_id` unique, `person_tenant` NOT NULL) and `Role` (`role_id` unique, `role_tenant` NOT NULL).
- Role → capability/trust mapping anticipates `contracts/eso/eso.schema.yaml` Block 5 `executorPolicy.trustLevels`.

## Related Graph Nodes

- `Person` (id, tenantId) — primary.
- `Role` (id, tenantId) — primary.
- Relationships: `Person` → `Role`; `Role` → `OrgUnit` (scope).

## Related APIs

- `api/` (Laravel — auth, tenancy, REST) — identity and role CRUD; the only entry point for identity operations.
- Consumed by `web/` user directory and role management screens.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. `Person` and `Role` records can be created and managed within a tenant via `api/`.
2. Every `Person`/`Role` node carries a non-null `tenantId`.
3. Roles can be assigned to persons (optionally unit-scoped) and resolve to a permission + trust ceiling.
4. Identity operations are only reachable through the `api/` auth/tenancy surface.
