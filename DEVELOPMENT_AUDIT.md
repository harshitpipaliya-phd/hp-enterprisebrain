## 1. Audit Scope

The full repository was scanned: engineering packages (`contracts/`, `graph/`, `api/`, `events/`, `ai/`, `web/`, `infra/`, `reference/`, `.github/`) and the Product Development Workspace (`development/`).

## 2. Conventions Observed (must be followed by any new artifact)

- `contracts/` is the ONLY source of truth; types are GENERATED (never hand-written) into `contracts/dist/`.
- `graph/` migrations run in filename order; every node MUST carry `tenantId` (exit criterion #6, enforced by `.github/workflows/tenant-isolation.yml`).
- Ledgers are append-only: Evidence, ReasoningStep, Task, Outcome, Learning. Current state = latest event.
- Every ingested fact carries provenance (source, system, method, timestamp, confidence, agent, type, version, hash) and cannot be backfilled.
- The product layer references engineering assets by link; it does not duplicate field-level definitions.
- `web/` design system CONSUMES `ESOContract` — it never defines it (see `web/MIGRATION.md`).

## 8. Risk Summary

1. **HIGH:** The "Engineering Blueprint" is cited as an authority but does not exist in-repo. Recommend importing it or re-pointing references to `contracts/eso/eso.schema.yaml` §5.2/§5.5 (the in-repo authority that already contradicts the Blueprint).
2. **MEDIUM:** Runtime + ledger graph models (`eso-runtime.schema.yaml`, `ReasoningStep`, `Task`) are named but absent. EPIC-005/008 depend on them.
3. **LOW (expected):** `api/`, `events/`, `ai/`, `infra/`, `openapi/`, `reference/` are scaffolded — consistent with "Sprint 1 = foundation only". Product specs correctly treat them as future dependencies, not defects.

## 9. Verdict

The repository is internally consistent at the foundation level. The product workspace is fully traceable to the engineering assets that exist. The only genuinely broken reference is the missing Engineering Blueprint document. All other "missing" items are scaffolded packages that are planned but not yet implemented — correctly referenced by the product layer as dependencies.
